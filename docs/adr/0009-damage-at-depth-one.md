# ADR-0009: Offensive families damage at depth one; iron is the support family

Date: 2026-07-22
Status: accepted

## Context

Each process family's signature hazard sits at the end of a synthesis chain measured from the
feedstocks sites actually supply: nitric acid is four reactions deep, hypochlorite and HCl two to
three. A new player pays the full chain — including its hidden activation conditions — before the
family produces any defense, which playtesting identifies as the largest single source of opacity.
The supply system already delivers normally-intermediate species directly (steam, carbon monoxide,
hydrogen fluoride), proving direct supply is content authoring rather than engine work. The iron
family has no damaging species at all: its solids carry no damage source, and its chemistry
shuttles oxygen between gas fronts.

## Decision

Every offensive family — chlorine-sodium, carbon-steam, nitrogen-oxide, nickel, and
uranium-fluorine — delivers a working hazard from at most one reaction or direct exposure on a
site-supplied feedstock. Sites supply intermediate species directly (ammonia, nitrogen dioxide,
chlorine, hypochlorite, hydrochloric acid) as priced, capped reservoir contents. Synthesis chains
become the family's economy: running the chain makes the feedstock free and scalable, while bought
feedstock stays priced and capped, so chain mastery is an optimization rather than an entry fee.

Iron is the game's explicit support family. Its promise is carriage and economy — moving chemical
potential between rooms and recycling feeds — and it is labeled as support in palettes, the
encyclopedia, and its spec plates. The depth-one promise applies to the five offensive families.

## Alternatives considered

- **Give iron a heat-damage identity through exothermic reoxidation** — keeps the depth-one
  promise uniform across all six families, but manufactures a combat role for a family whose real
  chemistry is carriage; rejected to keep the family's identity honest.
- **Merge iron into carbon-steam** — the families already couple through CO and CO₂, but merging
  shrinks palette variety to five families and buries the carrier-loop archetype inside another
  family's identity.
- **Keep chains as entry requirements with better presentation** — richer chain visualization
  leaves the multi-reaction entry fee in place; rejected as insufficient.

## Consequences

- Currently product-only species gain supply-content definitions with matching reservoir phases;
  liquid intermediates require liquid reservoir assignments.
- Supply pricing and replenishment caps become a per-site difficulty and economy dial, and the
  balance workbook solves family capacity from supplied feedstocks as well as synthesized ones.
- The campaign contract's "efficient direct counter" requirement becomes reachable at every site
  through the palette's supplied feedstocks.
- Iron's exemption is a documented, player-visible classification rather than a silent gap.
